Who is Grace Hopper?

She is a computer programmer and a rear admiral .

She developed a programming language called COBOL .

COBAL is designed with business users in mind and helped many people in various business fields .

Her Early Life

Grace Brewster Murray was born on December 9th, 1906 in New York. She was always very curious as a child, a trait she kept for her whole life. At the age of seven, she wanted to figure out how an alarm clock worked, and took apart 7 clocks before her mother realised what she was doing! (She was then given a single clock to work with.)

Her Navy Career

At the US's entry to World War II, Grace tried to enlist in the Navy, but was rejected for 3 main reasons:

  • She was 34 years old and too old to enlist.
  • She was wasn't heavy enough for her height
  • She was a maths professor at Vassar College, and was considered too valuable to the war effort.
Despite this, she would later (In 1943) go on leave from the College to volunteer for the US Navy Reserve. (Even then, she had to get a special exemption, as she was still around 7kg underweight for the Navy!)

She reported for duty in December of that year, and trained in the Smith College Naval Reserve School in Massachusetts.

She then went on do graduate the next year, and then served as a lieutenant on the Buerau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard, working on the Harvard Mark 1 (A computer that used moveing parts instead of modern silicon boards!)

Here she seemed to develop a love for computers, as at the end of the war she chose to continue working at the Mark 1 lab, until 1949. (she even turned down a full Professorship at Vassar College, to work as a researcher at Harvard on computers.)
